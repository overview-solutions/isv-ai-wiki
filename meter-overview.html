Metering focus

IEEE ISV Tech Comm · primary endeavor · Jun 2026

Current primary endeavor Tech Comm is concentrating volunteer effort on affordable, interoperable village metering — multi-vendor normalization at the gateway and lightweight standards that avoid utility AMI cost. Pole EMS, OpenAMI, and other initiatives continue, but metering is the active coordination thread for standards, vendor outreach, and anchor developer POCs.

Funded mini-grids repeatedly hit the same wall: metering capex rivals generation hardware, operators cannot bill or catch theft without per-customer reads, and protocol fragmentation (STS, DLMS, OEM mesh) blocks swap and scale.

Two goals, one program

Multi-vendor normalization

Anchor developers and operators should not be locked to one OEM NAN or proprietary cloud API forever.

  • Northbound JSON/MQTT export at the gateway — v0.1 draft
  • VMRS register checklist + vendor ICD mapping — OBIS · VMRS
  • Anchor-led feeder POCs with published gap matrices

Lightweight, less expensive systems

Villages should not pay for MDMS, full Profile Generic, or certification surfaces they will never use.

  • Scope-limited procurement language (village vs utility layers)
  • Cited $/connection bands — vendor study
  • STS/SMS and redundant field paths when DCU fails

Why this matters (problems summary)

  • Cadillac procurement on village budgets — RFPs copy utility DLMS/MDMS language; vendors quote full AMI when operators only need prepaid + feeder visibility.
  • Layer confusion — STS (payment), OBIS (semantics), DLMS (protocol), and SunSpec (DER) get conflated in one compliance column.
  • OEM lock-in — proprietary RF and gated ICDs block second-source DCUs and multi-vendor feeders.
  • Comms fragility — when the DCU or WAN fails, billing must still work via STS/SMS and CIU paths.
