Workshop vision

To bring a fresh perspective beyond previous cloud-centric hackathons — and in the spirit of IEEE Smart Village technology workshops — this IEEE ISV track anchors on the open-source Street-EMS platform (MeshEMS v2.0 · meshems-openami-metering).

Unlike past hackathons, the workshop introduces business-case sensitivity alongside engineering. Participants explore both open and commercial aggregation and backend architectures supported by Street-EMS, including:

  • DLMS / COSEM — utility-grade meter semantics southbound
  • EnAccess — multi-tenant REST API / OpenAMI operator stack
  • OpenEMS — rapidly maturing open-source aggregation and energy management framework

Audience: in-country engineers developing village electrification and community minigrid solutions. Format: mentoring-based Make · Code · Integrate — three sessions across the conference week.

In keeping with the IEEE ISV mission, solution architecture cost sensitivity is a first-class design criterion — scoped to what operators can actually deploy, operate, and sustain in-country.

Make · Code · Integrate

MAKE — Monday morning (4 hr) Build a Street-EMS controller; integrate it into a three-phase Street-EMS reference cabinet with DIN-rail breakers, contactor, and multi-tenant metering options (single-phase and three-phase cabinet variants).
CODE — Tuesday evening (2 hr) Code-challenge portions: network Street-EMS with alternative cloud and cloudless backend architectures — EnAccess MPM, OpenEMS paths, DLMS/COSEM integration labs.
INTEGRATE — Saturday morning (2 hr) Evaluate, discuss, and build out a decision matrix — technical and commercial trade-offs of each aggregation approach (deployment cost, scalability, sovereignty, communications requirements, lifecycle sustainability).

Cloudless · sovereign architecture option

The workshop introduces a truly cloudless, sovereign village electrification path as an evolution of Street-EMS — embedded LV feeder distributed intelligence:

  • Measure everywhere — actionable telemetry at feeder, branch, and customer
  • Policy-based, telecom-like session admission — asset transfer and peer-to-peer control-plane options
  • Transmit only actionable telemetry — not utility-scale archive dumps
  • Street-EMS intelligence at the LV feeder — shifting the LV feeder from the traditional “last kilometre” to the first kilometre of community-owned, villager-empowered, resilient electrification

Connects to ISV OpenAMI and OBIS · VMRS northbound normalization threads.

Trade · logistics · in-country manufacturing

The workshop also covers value-added impact of recent cross-Africa multi-country free trade agreement (AfCFTA) opportunities for in-country startups — awareness and proper use of Harmonized System (HS) codes for module and PCB import, local assembly, test, programming, repair, and export.

Decision matrix criteria

INTEGRATE session drafts a practical matrix comparing aggregation approaches on:

  • Deployment cost — capex and in-country assembly
  • Scalability — feeder growth and multi-tenant expansion
  • Sovereignty — data residency and operator control
  • Communications requirements — always-on cloud vs periodic sync vs offline-first
  • Lifecycle sustainability — repair, HS import/export, local programming and test
