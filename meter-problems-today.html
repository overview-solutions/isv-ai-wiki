Problems to solve

What village and mini-grid operators need from metering — and how ISV is helping

A rural utility with 200 customers cannot run like a city grid with 500,000 meters. Operators still need to bill fairly, cut off non-payers, and find stolen power — without spending more on meters than on solar panels. Who we mean by “village” · cited population and access context. Below: field problems and practical approaches.

Simple data path: Customer pays → credit reaches the meter → meter tracks use → pole box reads the meter → cloud gets one standard JSON report → billing software updates. Payment codes (prepaid tokens) travel on a separate path from meter reads — usually SMS or a keypad — and that is normal.

Billing & payment

ProblemWhy it hurtsApproaches we're tracking
Know how much each customer used Flat village tariffs hide freeloaders; you cannot bill fairly without a per-home kWh read. Revenue meter at each connection; minimum register checklist starts with cumulative import kWh.
Collect payment before or as energy is used Post-paid rural accounts go unpaid; operators need cash flow to fix lines and fuel gensets. Prepaid meters; vendor study scores token-capable models; MicroPowerManager for accounts and mobile money.
Deliver prepaid credit to the meter Customer paid at a shop or on a phone — energy must still reach their meter. 20-digit token entry at meter or customer interface unit (wall keypad); SMS/USSD vending; industry-standard token format (STS) or open OpenPAYGO.
Show remaining balance Households need to know when to buy more; support calls drop when balance is visible locally. CIU display or vendor app; northbound read of credit register where meter exposes it — see register checklist.
Audit who received which token Disputes and fraud investigations need a last-token trail. Vendor-dependent audit register; optional tier in VMRS; vending system logs from operator CRM.
Keep the grid funded (O&M revenue) Without steady collections, lines stay broken and diesel runs out. Reliable prepaid + at least one anchor business on the feeder paying year-round; see Circles of Power notes.

Cutoffs & control

ProblemWhy it hurtsApproaches we're tracking
Disconnect non-paying customers Physical pole climbs are expensive and dangerous; credit exhausted should mean power off. Internal relay in smart meter; remote disconnect command over meter protocol; register checklist includes disconnect state.
Reconnect after payment Truck rolls for reconnect kill revenue; should be automatic once credit is restored. Same relay + token or remote reconnect from billing platform; test in vendor POC before fleet buy.
Control several homes from one pole Pole-mounted distribution is common; one box can host multiple meters and relays. Street Pole EMS prototype — multiple meters + relays per pole; MeshEMS board for lab and pilot builds.

Theft, leakage & losses

ProblemWhy it hurtsApproaches we're tracking
Illegal taps and bypasses Energy leaves the feeder without passing a meter; honest customers subsidize thieves. Tamper and cover-open alarms on revenue meters; per-customer reads vs feeder total; field patrol triggered by alarms.
Unmetered load on the feeder Aggregate feeder meter minus sum of customers reveals hidden use — if you have both reads. Feeder-level CT + customer meters; OpenAMI telemetry at the edge; pole EMS aggregation.
Continuous leakage on low-voltage lines Faulty insulation or equipment draws power 24/7; hard to spot with monthly reads only. Residual-current monitoring at feeder edge; OSEAS 2026 challenge theme (leakage detection on pole EMS); on-demand power reads for anomaly flags.
Neutral imbalance on three-phase feeders Voltage sag, overheated neutrals, unhappy customers — common on village LV networks. Feeder-edge monitoring + alerts; alternate OSEAS challenge theme (neutral balancing).
Two meters disagree Which read do you bill? Drift >5% needs investigation before trusting revenue data. Calibration and install QA; compare against feeder total; field note from India IPP partners.
Spot theft without expensive utility analytics Full utility load-profile databases cost too much for 200 meters. Instantaneous power read when suspicion arises; optional interval reads only if you have an analytics plan; lighter NILM (signature disaggregation) explored at 2025 hackathon.

Visibility & operations

ProblemWhy it hurtsApproaches we're tracking
Voltage problems at the home Brownouts damage appliances and hint at overloaded feeders. Voltage register on revenue meter; alarm thresholds in billing or EMS software.
See power use right now Theft and overload show up in watts before they show up in monthly kWh. Instantaneous power in register checklist; northbound JSON on change or poll.
Trust meter time and identity Wrong clock corrupts billing periods; wrong serial bills the wrong house. Clock sync at gateway; serial number in every northbound message — JSON profile v0.1.

Affordability

ProblemWhy it hurtsApproaches we're tracking
Meters cost as much as generation Legacy prepaid installs at $600–1,500 per home can exceed solar hardware spend. Target ~$58/household pole EMS path vs legacy meter; industry bands $40–110 cited in vendor study; buy only registers you need.
Utility-grade software priced for utilities Full billing and MDMS suites assume millions of meters, not hundreds. Open operator tools (MPM); minimum cloud data contract instead of full AMI stack — see overview.

When communications fail

ProblemWhy it hurtsApproaches we're tracking
Cloud cannot reach meters today Rural GPRS, mesh, and pole uplinks fail often; operator still blind to field state. Redundant radio paths (RF mesh + PLC); May 2026 topology notes; proposed phone-as-relay uplink when pole gateway is down.
Prepaid still works but office does not know Meters keep consuming local credit when link is down; CIU may hold stats cloud wants. STS tokens via SMS without live meter feedback; sync CIU or meter when link returns; do not assume always-on cloud reads.
Customer cannot buy credit because network is down Token vending should not require the same broken path as scheduled meter reads. Separate SMS/USSD vending channel; local token entry at CIU; redundant channel table.

Integration & long-term freedom

ProblemWhy it hurtsApproaches we're tracking
Hook billing to mobile money and CRM Operators run on M-Pesa and spreadsheets; meter data must land in one place. Northbound REST/MQTT to operator platform; EnAccess MPM; vendor cloud where acceptable — score APIs in vendor study.
Every meter brand speaks a different language Swapping vendor means rewriting integrations. Pole gateway reads vendor protocol locally, publishes one JSON/MQTT shape northbound — mixed fleets on one feeder.
Vendor exit strands installed meters Closed mesh + cloud bundles block migration. Open northbound contract; document gaps in POC matrices; OpenAMI roadmap.
Confuse customer meters with solar inverter monitoring DER monitoring (generation) is a different job from revenue metering (billing). Separate procurement columns; inverter data via SunSpec track — not mixed into prepaid token path.
Where ISV fits

IEEE Smart Village does not sell meters. The Tech Committee publishes draft checklists and test results so operators, integrators, and vendors argue from the same field problems — then cite evidence in vendor study and anchor pilots (SteamaCo, Darway). See overview for how ISV fits.
