Problems we are trying to solve

Rural mini-grid operators often cannot afford per-customer AMI. Without metering they cannot bill reliably, detect theft, or maintain systems. Below the feeder, devices speak STS, DLMS, OEM, MODBUS, and proprietary RF — while utilities and aggregators need IEEE 2030.5 and SunSpec-shaped data. The 2025 hack tested open pole-mount EMS hardware and protocol gateways against these constraints.

What came out of the hackathon

  • Street Pole EMS Box (ISV + NESL + Energy IOT) — ESP32, three meters + relays per pole, SunSpec JSON over MQTT; branches on github.com/energy-iot/ems-dev
  • Feeder-level DER market — Post Road Foundation: geofence is the distribution feeder, not the whole utility territory
  • Anchor customers — ISV grant requirement; a paying business stabilizes village O&M (maps to field reality, not metaphor)
  • NILM — aggregate signature disaggregation as a path to lighter-weight telemetry
