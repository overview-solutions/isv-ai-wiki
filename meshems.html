MeshEMS board

NESL · OpenAMI-aligned · multi-tenant edge metering · workshops & field deployments

Board diagram

The MeshEMS board is an ESP32-based EMS PCB designed by NESL in alignment with OpenAMI — mesh-capable neighborhood-area networking for DCU backhaul when pole-to-pole RF redundancy is needed. Current OpenAMI metering firmware targets the NESL 865B board: meshems-openami-metering.

OpenAMI alignment Board layout and I/O support OpenAMI field deployments: Modbus RTU meters (DDS238, CHD130, DDSU666) or CircuitSetup ATM90E32 6-channel SPI metering, MQTT northbound telemetry, and SSR load control. Schematic: nesl-meshems · application firmware: meshems-openami-metering.

Why this board exists

The MeshEMS dev kit is a multi-tenant edge metering system — one pole gateway that can serve several customer circuits (~6 homes or loads) while staying honest about what belongs on each side of the meter. It is used across workshops, hackathons, and field deployments (including the Power Africa 2026 workshop, Open Energy hackathons, and operator pilots such as Jude’s 10× EMS scale-up). The board is not only hardware; it is a learning exercise in working with real off-the-shelf power electronics and energy metering gear, documented in the open so communities can reuse the lessons.

Goal: help small communities develop technological autonomy early — especially during periods of rapid load growth — by curating clear understanding of the metering ecosystem, sharing how systems connect, and building cohesion across vendors and open stacks instead of locking operators into one proprietary AMI bundle.

Upstream · meter · downstream

A practical first step is to clarify and isolate knowledge on each side of the revenue meter. MeshEMS sits at that boundary and makes interoperability visible — what the DCU must speak upstream versus what each customer circuit needs downstream.

Upstream of the meter

  • DCUs and pole gateways
  • Utility / feeder systems
  • Head-end, vending, operator backends
  • Northbound MQTT · geo asset model

Downstream of the meter

  • Multiple homes and customer circuits (~6 per board)
  • Per-tenant reads, credit, disconnect
  • Branch loads, anchor tenants, theft visibility
  • Off-the-shelf meters and SSR control

← revenue meter / gateway normalization →

Workshop and field kits exercise both sides: southbound OEM and Modbus paths into the board, northbound registers operators can bill on. Documenting that split — in hardware, firmware, and wiki — is how ISV helps operators and integrators find where systems actually meet.

OpenAMI · OBIS · building in the open

OpenAMI is the collaborative stack MeshEMS aligns with — actionable telemetry, leakage at feeder → branch → customer, vendor-agnostic gateways. OBIS · VMRS gives shared register semantics on the northbound path so the same JSON contract works across meter brands. MeshEMS is one concrete place those abstractions meet physical wiring.

ISV’s role: continue to curate understanding of this ecosystem — workshop notes, gap matrices, board diagrams, distribution lists — and work in alignment with EnAccess, Energy IoT, NESL, and field operators so cohesion grows from shared practice, not from a single vendor’s closed stack.

MeshEMS vs Street Pole EMS

Street Pole EMS — single-pole ESP32 gateway prototyped at the 2025 Open Energy Hackathon; live metering, SunSpec telemetry, MQTT northbound.

MeshEMS — NESL PCB variant with mesh backhaul so multiple DCUs can form a neighborhood-area network when line-of-sight or single-hop RF is insufficient. Use when the OpenAMI deployment needs redundant pole-to-pole links, not when a standalone pole gateway is enough.

Power rails (workshop)

Multiple supplies must be planned on the bench and in the field — 5 V for temperature reference and SSRs; 3.3 V for CircuitSetup stack logic; buck/boost from 18650 packs as needed. Full rail table: 25 Jun 2026 notes · MeshEMS power architecture.
